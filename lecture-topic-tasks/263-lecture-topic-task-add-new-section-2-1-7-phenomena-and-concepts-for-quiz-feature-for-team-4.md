According to the lecture material on Phenomena and Concepts (phenomena-concepts.pdf), a phenomenon is an observable, concrete instance that can be sensed or measured, while a concept is an abstraction or class that represents a set of related phenomena. This issue focuses on identifying and classifying the phenomena and concepts present in the quiz algorithm in the creation of a new subsection 2.1.7 "Phenomena and Concepts for Quiz Feature". The new subsection will identify and categorize the entities, functions, and events/behaviors that make up the quiz, describing how concrete, measurable elements (phenomena) relate to abstract structures (concepts).

The analysis will include:

- Entities: Concepts that carry data, such as Word, IPA, Syllables, OptionSet, and QuizState.
- Functions: Operations that act on those entities, such as Get_Word_IPA(word) or Generate_Evil_Words(word).
- Events and behaviors: Observable actions during quiz execution, such as WordRequested, OptionsGenerated, and OptionSelected.
